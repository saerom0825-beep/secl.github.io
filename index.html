About

The Smart Environmental Chemistry Lab focuses on understanding the fate, transport, remediation, and source tracking of emerging contaminants in water, soil and groundwater environments. Our research integrates AI-based approaches to predict the distribution of emerging contaminants, optimize water treatment technologies, and assess risk indices for sustainable environmental management.

Research Areas

Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS)

1. Development of pretreatment methods for PFAS analysis 2. Identification of unknown PFAS compounds 3. Evaluation of oxidative and reductive treatment technologies

Microplastics (MPs)

1. Development of reliable pretreatment and analytical methods 2. Investigation of the fate of MPs in environmental samples

CO₂ Capture in Soil Systems

1. Construction of soil column systems to investigate CO₂ capture, transport, and stabilization in subsurface environments

AI-based Environmental Chemistry

1. Machine learning-based prediction of micropollutant occurrence 2. Source attribution for PFAS 3. Optimization for treatment technologies for emerging contaminants
