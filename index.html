About

The Smart Environmental Chemistry Lab focuses on understanding the fate, transport, treatment, and source attribution of emerging contaminants in water, soil, groundwater, and subsurface environments. Our research integrates advanced analytical chemistry, environmental experiments, and AI-based data analysis to support sustainable environmental management.

Research Areas

PFAS Analysis & Treatment

Development of pretreatment methods for PFAS analysis and evaluation of oxidative and reductive treatment technologies.

Microplastics Analysis

Development of reliable pretreatment and analytical methods for microplastics in environmental samples.

CO₂ Capture & Soil Column Systems

Construction of soil column systems to investigate CO₂ capture, transport, and stabilization in subsurface environments.

AI-based Environmental Chemistry

Machine learning-based prediction, source attribution, data interpretation, and optimization for emerging contaminants.
