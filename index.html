About

The Smart Environmental Chemistry Lab focuses on understanding the fate, transport, remediation, and source tracking of emerging contaminants in water, soil and groundwater environments. Our research integrates AI-based approaches to predict the distribution of emerging contaminants, optimize water treatment technologies, and assess risk indices for sustainable environmental management.

Research Areas

Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS)

Development of pretreatment methods, identification of unknown PFAS compounds, and evaluation of oxidative and reductive treatment technologies.

Microplastics (MPs)

Development of reliable pretreatment and analytical methods and investigation of the fate of microplastics in environmental samples.

CO₂ Capture in Soil Systems

Construction of soil column systems to investigate CO₂ capture, transport, and stabilization in subsurface environments.

AI-based Environmental Chemistry

Machine learning-based prediction of micropollutant occurrence, source attribution of PFAS, and optimization of treatment technologies for emerging contaminants.
